asking the right questions the right way

data analysis

getting the relevant information out of it

  • perform for you

    • data analysis (descriptive, inferential)
    • data management (eg., cleaning and preparation)

  • includes

    • communication of data and analysis to the researcher
      • report and R-code (supporting reproducible research)
        • with visualizations, summaries
        • with discussions on choices and inference
      • meeting (consulting)

data analysis - practicalities

getting the relevant information out of it

  • typically time consuming: a few days to a few weeks
  • researchers are expected to participate
    • translate substantive interests into the language of statisticians
    • provide data that is easy to understand and deal with
    • show appropriate skepticism to ensure analyses are correct
    • try to understand and learn for future research
  • as co-author, consultants take responsibility
    • support in writing the statistical part of the manuscript
    • dealing with reviewer comments

data analysis - case 1

Flamée, P., Varnavas, V., Dewals, W., Carvalho, H., Cools, W., Bhutia, J. T., Beckers, S., Umbrain, V., Verborgh, C., Forget, P., Chierchia, G.-B., Brugada, P., Poelaert, J., & de Asmundis, C. (2020). Electrocardiographic Effects of Propofol versus Etomidate in Patients with Brugada Syndrome. Anesthesiology, 132, 440–451.

  • major revision, primarily due to statistics
  • removed all statistics
  • enhanced story, through visualizations
  • no clear indication of systematic change

data analysis - case 2

Smeets, S., Stangé, G., Leuckx, G., Roelants, L., Cools, W., De Paep, D. L., Ling, Z., De Leu, N., & in’t Veld, P. (2020). Evidence of Tissue Repair in Human Donor Pancreas After Prolonged Duration of Stay in Intensive Care. Diabetes, 69, 401-412.

  • refined existing analysis
  • introduced permutation tests
  • included visualizations
  • differences with time 0 exist

data analysis - case 3

Baert, Y., Ruetschle, I., Cools, W., Lorenz, A., Marx, U., Oehme, A., Goossens, E. and Maschmeyer, I. (in press). A multi-organ-chip co-culture of liver and testis equivalents: a first step toward a systemic male reprotoxicity model. Human Reproduction.

  • dealing with
    • repeated measurements
    • deviating 1st measure
    • small sample sizes
    • multiple conditions
  • modeling evolution over time with mixed models
  • various conclusions drawn
    • on differences in evolution
    • on differences at end

data analysis - case 4

Gilissen, J., Gastmans, C., Wendrich-van Dael, A., Cools, W., Vander Stichele, R., Deliens, L., Van den Block, L. & Pivodic, L. (accepted). Nurses’ self-efficacy, rather than their knowledge, is associated with their engagement in advance care planning in nursing homes: a survey study. Palliative Medicine.

  • reports for communication
  • R-code for reproducibility
